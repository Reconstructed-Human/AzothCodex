Six stages. Three centuries of working. One instrument the operation has always required.

Michael Snyder

Glenn Redyns is a botanical alchemist. Forty-two years old. Unremarkable by most accounts. He does not know that his specific cognitive architecture was anticipated three hundred years ago, that a city's geography was marked in 1958 with him in mind, or that the operation running beneath the surface of everything has always been waiting for the right instrument.

The Azoth Codex traces the Great Work through six stages. Each book is a transformation. None of them are reversible.
